This essay explores the continuing relevance of the Indian epics *Mahabharata* and *Ramayana* in modern society.  This essay argues that these epics cannot be reduced to moral binaries. Instead, they should be understood through the interplay of karma (action) and dharma (consequence) , while distinguishing dhamma (renunciant path) from dharma (householder duty). Through episodes of Rāvaṇa, Bhīṣma, and Sītā, the essay shows how these ancient texts continue to illuminate modern ethical dilemmas.

It discusses concepts such as **karma**, **dharma**, and the ethical dilemmas presented in the epics.
